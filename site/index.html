Why Adaptive π is different

Traditional CAD breaks curves into triangles. Adaptive π keeps them analytic, letting you design in 4D with pristine curvature, time-aware operations, and exports that scale indefinitely.

FAQ

Answers to the most common questions from backers and press.

What does “4D” mean here?

Adaptive π handles time, fields, and higher-grade geometry alongside spatial dimensions. You can model dynamic systems without collapsing to static meshes.

How heavy is the tool?

The alpha runs on modern Apple, NVIDIA, Intel, and AMD hardware. Analytic rendering is CPU-first with GPU upgrades mapped post-campaign.

Which formats are supported?

AMA / ACProj natively with bridges to IFC, LandXML, and STL repair. Blender + FreeCAD plugins are on the Creator tier roadmap.

Can I use my own footage?

Yes — drop hero.mp4 and an optional hero_poster.jpg in site/assets/. The deploy workflow keeps binaries out of git unless you opt-in.
